The Genomics Virtual Laboratory takes the IT out of Bioinformatics. It provides a cloud-based suite of genomics analysis tools that would normally require specialist assistance. Refer to workbench services for full details, or this brochure for an overview.

Development Partners

Our developers are contributing to many aspects of the GVL. Many others are also contributing knowledge, advice and assistance to our team members.
  • The University of QLD Research Computing Centre is the Lead Agent, is developing GVL infrastructure on the QLD Research Cloud with QCIF, and developing researcher resources with QFAB:
    • The University of Queensland (RCC)
    • Queensland Facility for Advanced Bioinformatics (QFAB)
    • Queensland Cyber Infrastructure Foundation (QCIF)
  • Life Sciences Computation Centre at the VLSCI is developing GVL infrastructure on the Melbourne Research Cloud with University of Melbource ITS, and developing researcher resources with Peter Mac and Baker IDI:
    • Life Sciences Computation Centre at the VLSCI (LSCC)
    • Victorian eResearch Strategic Initiative (VeRSI)
    • The University of Melbourne (UoM)
    • Baker IDI Heart and Diabetes Institute (Baker IDI)
    • Peter MacCallum Cancer Centre (PeterMac)
  • Garvan Institute of Medical Research is developing and operating Galaxy in house, and is contributing expertise and researcher resources:
    • The Garvan Institute of Medical Research (Garvan)
    • Victor Chang Cardiac Research Institute
  • CSIRO is developing and operating Galaxy in house, so is contributing know how and expertise, and developing researcher resources:
    • CSIRO
  • CSIRO and BioPlatforms Australia are integrating framework datasets into the GVL:
    • CSIRO
    • Bioplatforms Australia (BPA)
  • Several other Universities are planning to develop the GVL as further Research Cloud nodes come on line:
    • Monash University
    • The University of Sydney
    • The University of Western Australia
  • The project is receiving further support and encouragement from:
    • Australian Bioinformatics Network
    • EMBL Australia
    • Australian Genome Research Facility (AGRF)
    • Australian National Data Service (ANDS)
